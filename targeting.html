UnderDefense  ///  Who we target, and why
PREPARED BY IMPACTABLE  //  JUNE 2026  //  WARM-START  //  LINKEDIN + GOOGLE

This worksheet maps the audiences UnderDefense will target across LinkedIn and Google, ordered by the efficiency ladder: warm first, ABM second, cold last. Every layer ties back to the white-space wedge — published pricing, included incident response and compliance, humans who contain the threat, and six years of proof. The headline call: this is a warm start. We do not begin with cold.

Paid Search — High-Intent Keywords

Google is the demand-capture engine, not demand gen. The account's own history confirms it: brand + competitor terms were the only thing working, and high-intent ads got starved when budget was diluted across high-volume terms. So we go narrow, high-intent, exact/phrase match, with a hard negative-keyword firewall.

Start here (5–6 themes, ~$5–6K/mo)

Brand defense + MDR pricing / SOC cost (our published-pricing wedge wins these outright) + managed SOC / MDR services + the top two competitor-alternative themes (Arctic Wolf, Expel). Single-theme ad groups, exact and phrase match, each pointed at a dedicated landing page. Do not dilute into high-volume education terms — that is exactly what starved the winners last time.

Negative-keyword firewall (mandatory)

Jobs/careers (jobs, salary, certification, cissp) · Free/DIY (free soc, open source siem, build your own soc) · Education (course, training, tutorial, what is) · Consumer/AV (antivirus, home security, personal vpn) · Defense/military/government (client exclusion) · Wrong "SOC" (system on chip, soc code, sons of) · Students (thesis, research paper) · Staffing/MSP-resale. Mirror active keywords as DSA negatives to prevent cannibalization.
